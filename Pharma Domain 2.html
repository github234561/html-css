Drug Discovery

This is the first phase and includes identifying the lead compound(chemical/biological compound) which fits in the target and cures a particular disease. Drug is a chemical substance that interacts with the proteins in the body to affect physiological functions.
Types of drugs:
Oral (tables, capsules, syrup)
Dermatology (cream, ointment)
Parenterals (Intravenous, Intramuscular)

Pre-Clinical Trials

This phase includes testing the safety and efficacy on animals.

Clinical trials

Clinical trials in turn include three phases
Phase 1
Conducted on healthy volunteers
Phase 2
Conducted on limited number of patients (hundreds in number)
Phase 3
Conducted on larger number of patients from across the globe.
Note that in each of the above phases safety and efficacy are the primary concerns.

Post Marketing Surveillance

This is when the safety of a drug is monitored after being released in the market.
Pharmacovigilance : Watching the effects of drugs at this point to understand any adverse reactions due to the drug.

Interesting innovations in cancer treatment

Immunotherapy: Makes the patient's immune system stronger enabling it to act on the tumor cells. For the CAR(Chimeric antigen receptor) T cells treatment, immune cells from a patient's bloodstream are taken and are reformed in such a way that they can attack a particular protein present in the cancer cells. The immune cells are re- injected into the patient's body, where the cells would act on the tumor cells.
This method is less toxic and more specific to the patients compared to chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy etc. Also, the results have shown remarkable success for B cell malignancies.
